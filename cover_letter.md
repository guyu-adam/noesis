# Cover Letter

**Manuscript:** What Coalition Broadcast Reveals About Causal Integration: A Diagnostic Toolkit and Empirical Constraints from a GWT--IIT Neural Processor System

**Journal:** Entropy (MDPI)

---

Dear Editor,

I am submitting the above manuscript for consideration in Entropy. This paper presents a computational investigation of the relationship between global workspace broadcast mechanisms and integrated information ($\Phi$) in a recurrent neural processor system, together with a transferable diagnostic toolkit for computational $\Phi$ studies.

I am an independent researcher without institutional affiliation. The work was conducted on personal hardware (RTX 5060 Ti GPU). All code, experimental configurations, raw per-cycle data, and analysis scripts are publicly available on GitHub under the MIT license at `https://github.com/guyu-adam/noesis`. The specific experiment data reported in this manuscript correspond to commit `ef5a1ff`. Every number in the paper can be reproduced by running `python run_experiment.py` in the repository.

The contribution of this work is methodological rather than confirmatory. I do not claim that the RNN system is conscious, that the $\Phi$ approximation equals true IIT $\Phi$, or that any specific broadcast architecture is superior. Instead, the paper provides: (1) a diagnostic toolkit ($\Phi$ decomposition, attention weight tracking, coalition size monitoring, merge fidelity metrics) that proved its value by identifying two non-trivial implementation issues during development; (2) empirical constraints on what $\Phi_\mathrm{approx}$ measures in recurrent architectures---namely, that it is dominated by total recurrent neuron count, not broadcast mechanism; and (3) a concrete operationalization of the Maximum Caliber IIT--FEP bridge (Kearney 2026) into a coalition selection objective.

The manuscript went through an extensive development process. Two implementation issues---an effective information accumulation artifact and a coalition formation bug---were identified and corrected through the diagnostic tools developed in this work. The corrected results are reported transparently, including null results where the data warrant them. I believe this approach to honest reporting of null findings aligns with the methodological standards advocated by Ferrante et al. (2025) and the broader open science movement in computational neuroscience.

As an independent researcher without grant funding, I respectfully request a waiver of the article processing charge (APC) under Entropy's waiver policy for authors without institutional support.

Thank you for your consideration.
